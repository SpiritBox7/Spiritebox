Introduction to IT and ITES industry

1. Definition of ICT

ICT stands for Information and Communication Technology.

It is a broader term than IT (Information Technology) as it includes the integration of telecommunications (phones, wireless signals) and computers to allow users to access, store, transmit, and manipulate information.

Information Technology Enabled Services (ITeS)

2.ITeS refers to outsourcing processes that are enabled by IT to improve efficiency and reduce costs.

Common Areas: Finance, HR, healthcare, and telecommunications.

Examples: Medical transcription, data entry, CRM (Customer Relationship Management), and software development.

3. Business Process Outsourcing (BPO)

BPO is the contracting of specific business tasks to external service providers.

Services include: Taxation, legal services, multimedia, healthcare, and software testing.

BPM (Business Process Management): Focuses on managing and improving business processes to boost productivity and GDP.

4. Applications of ICT in Different Sectors

ICT has revolutionized various fields in 2025:

Education: Use of smart boards, e-learning platforms (like ePathshala), and Learning Management Systems (LMS).

Healthcare: Electronic Health Records (EHRs), MRI, CAT scans, and telemedicine.

Banking: Online banking, ATMs, NEFT, and RTGS.

Governance (e-Governance): Digital India initiatives, Aadhaar (UIDAI), and online railway bookings (IRCTC).

Everyday Life: Smartphones, social media (Facebook, Instagram), and smart home devices.

5. Basic Components of a Computer System

Hardware: Physical components like the keyboard, mouse, monitor, and CPU.

Software: Programs and instructions (e.g., Windows, MS Office) that tell the hardware what to do.

Central Processing Unit (CPU): Known as the "brain" of the computer. It includes:

ALU (Arithmetic Logic Unit): Performs mathematical and logical operations.

CU (Control Unit): Directs the operations of the processor.

MU (Memory Unit): Stores data and instructions.

6. Digital Communication Tools

Tools commonly used for collaboration include:

Communication: Skype, Slack, and email.

Storage & Collaboration: Dropbox and Google Drive.

7. Safety and Security

Precautions: Install antivirus software, use strong passwords, and avoid sharing sensitive data online.

Cyber Hygiene: Regularly update software and be cautious of harmful digital content.
